## Verifiable predictions

- Draymond predicts the Golden State Warriors will beat the LA Clippers in the NBA Play-In game.
  * **Roster health:** Al Horford, Steph Curry, and KP are all back; Warriors are as healthy as they'll be, which significantly strengthens their bigs.
  * **Experience:** Multiple Warriors (Draymond, Steph, Al, KP, GP) are NBA champions with deep playoff experience who know how to handle do-or-die games.
  * **Game plan confidence:** Coaching staff has built what Draymond calls a great game plan, and players have had two good practices in LA to get comfortable with it.
  * **Motivation to flip the script:** Warriors are 1-9 in their last 10 meetings with the Clippers, including 1-3 this season, which Draymond treats as motivation rather than a deterrent.

- Draymond predicts Shai Gilgeous-Alexander will win back-to-back NBA MVP awards.
  * **Best record:** Thunder hold the number-one record in the entire league.
  * **Consistent production:** SGA puts up 30 points a night and carries his team on a nightly basis.
  * **Burden of proof on challenger:** Draymond argues Wembanyama needs to "go take it" from an incumbent MVP rather than win a neck-and-neck race; he doesn't believe Wimby did enough this season.

- Draymond predicts Victor Wembanyama will win Defensive Player of the Year, likely unanimously.
  * **Defensive dominance:** Wembanyama's impact on the defensive end is described as equivalent to how elite players dominate offensively — blocks, presence, altering shots.
  * **Offensive mismatch too:** At 7'5" running off flares and shooting threes, he is a unique two-way force that elevates his team's ceiling.
  * **Team result:** He has the Spurs at the two seed in the West, validating his impact on winning.

- Draymond predicts Khanon Kupple (Charlotte Hornets) will win NBA Rookie of the Year over Cooper Flagg.
  * **Consistency all season:** Kupple had no significant slow stretch, staying productive from start to finish, unlike Cooper Flagg who got off to a very slow start.
  * **Production:** Averaged over 18 points and led the NBA in three-point shooting as a rookie.
  * **Impact on winning:** Draymond repeatedly cites winning as a key factor; Kupple helped transform the Charlotte Hornets into a play-in team — an outcome nobody predicted.

- Draymond predicts Mitch Johnson will win NBA Coach of the Year.
  * **Unexpected result:** Getting the San Antonio Spurs to the number-two seed in the West was something no one foresaw this quickly.
  * **Continuous improvement:** The Spurs kept getting better and better as the season went on.
  * **Legacy pressure:** Stepping into Gregg Popovich's shadow and not missing a beat is an underrated accomplishment.

- Draymond predicts Keldon Johnson will win NBA Sixth Man of the Year and should win it easily.
  * **Steady impact:** Widely spoken of within the Spurs organization as a consistent and steadying force, one of their main leaders despite coming off the bench.
  * **Team context:** Playing a key role on the number-two team in the West.
  * **Personal story:** Stayed through the Spurs' rebuild, lost his spot, battled back, and earned this recognition.

- Draymond predicts Nickeil Alexander-Walker will win Most Improved Player.
  * **Quantifiable breakout:** Broke the Atlanta Hawks' all-time single-season three-point record in his first year with the team.
  * **Impact on winning:** Helped the Hawks make the playoffs — the award should reflect impact on winning, not raw statistical jumps.
  * **Overcoming adversity:** Was traded away and discarded by multiple teams before having his best professional season.

- Draymond predicts Shai Gilgeous-Alexander will win Clutch Player of the Year.
  * **Late-season stretch:** SGA went on a run at the end of the season where he was gaming opposing teams over and over in clutch moments.
  * **Reliable closer:** Draymond frames him as the type of player you simply hand the ball to in clutch situations and he handles it himself.
