## Verifiable predictions

- Draymond predicts OKC Thunder sweep the Suns (win series 4-0), with the Suns failing to win another game.
  * **Series competitiveness:** Even without the controversial foul calls, the games haven't been competitive at all, so the disparity is real regardless of officiating complaints.
  * **Defensive strategy:** The only way Suns could stay close is to pack the paint and force OKC to shoot perimeter threes — but Draymond doesn't think that's happening.

- Draymond predicts the Nuggets come back from 3-1 down to win the series vs the T-Wolves, with the series going 7 games.
  * **Injuries decimating T-Wolves:** The T-Wolves lost their entire starting backcourt — Ant-Man out for the rest of the series (and multiple weeks) and Donte DiVincenzo to a torn Achilles — which makes overcoming a 3-1 lead very hard.
  * **Joker vs. length:** Rudy Gobert's length has been bothering Nikola Jokic, but Jokic can still hit those shots and could go for 40 any night.
  * **Historical parallel:** Length has historically been the one thing that troubles Jokic, as the 2020 Lakers proved, but Nuggets have won every series since.

- Draymond predicts the Pistons win Game 4 to tie the series 2-2 (series returns to Detroit at 2-2), with the Magic currently leading 2-1.
  * **Cade's resurgence:** Cade Cunningham came alive last game and needs to stay foot on the gas as the Pistons' only reliable offensive option.
  * **Jalen Duren must step up:** Duren has not played with the physicality he showed earlier in the season; Draymond believes he has the talent to flip the script.
  * **Defensive discipline:** Pistons need to stop chasing over screens on Franz Wagner and Paolo Banchero, and eliminate reaching fouls that put them in the penalty early.

- Draymond predicts the Lakers win the series vs the Rockets in 5 games (Lakers up 3-1, KD injured).
  * **LeBron's chess match dominance:** LeBron has been methodically walking the ball up and getting the Lakers whatever shot they want, and the Rockets have nobody capable of playing chess with him.
  * **KD unavailable:** Kevin Durant is out with a knee and ankle injury; without him the Rockets can't generate enough to extend the series.
  * **Home-court advantage:** Game 5 is in LA, where role players perform better, giving the Lakers an additional edge to close it out.

- Draymond predicts the Spurs win the series vs the Blazers in 5 games.
  * **Blazers blow big leads:** Two games in a row Portland built large leads only to squander them, losing both, indicating they can't maintain control down the stretch.
  * **No go-to playoff scorer:** The Blazers lack a proven "get-a-bucket" closer like Damian Lillard; Deni Avdija hasn't proved he can do that at the playoff level.
  * **Stephon Castle's impact:** Castle has been the most important player for the Spurs in this series, an exceptional two-way force who makes the team dramatically better.

- Draymond predicts the Cavs win Game 5 vs the Raptors (series tied 2-2, game in Cleveland).
  * **Home-court edge:** The Cavs are returning home where role players play better, and they remain in control of the series until Toronto proves otherwise.
  * **Raptors reeling:** Cleveland momentum favors the Cavs; if Toronto wants to shift the series they have to go on the road and win, which is very difficult.

- Draymond predicts the Knicks win the series vs the Hawks (series tied 2-2).
  * **Experience gap:** The Knicks have more playoff experience; the Hawks, despite having length and athleticism, still have more growing to do.
  * **JJ Redick needs a big game:** JJ needs to be more aggressive and get downhill, which hasn't happened enough — and if that doesn't materialize, the Knicks' depth wins out.
  * **Dyson Daniels liability:** Daniels is stifling Atlanta's offense by occupying a wing spot without enough offensive contribution, hampering JJ and JK's ability to attack downhill.

- Draymond predicts the Celtics win the series vs the 76ers in 5 games (Celtics up 3-1).
  * **Celtics know Joel:** Boston has had Philly's number for years and already knows exactly how to defend Embiid — incorporating him back into the starting lineup without reps just gives the Celtics a familiar look.
  * **No lineup continuity:** Embiid has only played about 25-30 games this season; the starting unit has zero continuity with him, making it hard to rely on him as a difference-maker right away.
  * **V.J. Edgecombe's diminished role:** Embiid's return drops Edgecombe from a near-second option to fourth option, undermining the lineup that was already competitive without Joel.
