## Summary

Draymond reflects on a stretch of games guarding elite scorers back-to-back — Kawhi
Leonard, Kevin Durant, and Shai Gilgeous-Alexander — and his philosophy on defending
superstars (disrupting their rhythm rather than "locking them up"). He recaps on-court
incidents from the Rockets game (the Jabari Smith technical, trash talk with KD at the
free-throw line) and praises playing alongside Al Horford. Around the league, he
celebrates Jayson Tatum's return after ~10 months from an Achilles rupture, pushes back
on Kenny Atkinson calling Derrick White a top-five player, and rips Daryl Morey's claim
that the three-point shot is worth too much as hypocritical. He closes with takes on the
Big 12's LED court, the Miami (OH) 31-0 NCAA tournament debate, and a Spartan Days
listener question about the 2010 tournament.
