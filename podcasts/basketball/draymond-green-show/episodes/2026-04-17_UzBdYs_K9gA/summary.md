## Verifiable predictions

- Draymond predicts the Boston Celtics will eliminate the Philadelphia 76ers in the first round of the 2026 NBA Playoffs.
  * **Historical precedent:** The 76ers have faced Boston five times in the playoffs since 2000 and lost every single one of those series.

## Summary

**Warriors playing-game win over the Clippers (124-119)**
Draymond recaps a back-and-forth game decided in the fourth quarter after Golden State trailed by 13. Key contributors: Gui Santos (big three to stop a Clipper run), Steph Curry (re-entered ~9-minute mark), Al Horford (down the stretch), Brandin Podziemski (early momentum). Draymond praises Steve Kerr extensively — his pregame and halftime messages, the in-game timeout sets, and the defensive game plan that assigned Draymond to Kawhi Leonard.

**Draymond on Kawhi Leonard**
Draymond describes guarding Kawhi as requiring total patience — any early lean or gamble is fatal because Kawhi is strong enough to bump through it and raise up for an uncontestable shot. He credits teammates for keeping the shell tight, enabling his two steals (one off a denied-outlet situation, one by timing a crossover he'd been anticipating all game). Kawhi finished with 21 points and commented post-game that it was "hard to even get shots up," which Draymond says meant a great deal coming from a fellow top-five defensive player.

**Warriors vs. Suns preview**
Next game is a playing-game elimination matchup against Phoenix. Draymond notes the Suns have multiple ball-handlers (Booker, Dillon Brooks having a career year, Jalen Green looking improved), all with the green light, and a physical pressing defense. He expects a competitive fight but expresses confidence in the Warriors when their backs are against the wall. He also notes a side implication: OKC receives the Clippers' pick and, if the Warriors advance, it moves from #12 to #11.

**NBA award eligibility — Luka and Cade made eligible**
The NBA ruled Luka Dončić and Cade Cunningham eligible for postseason awards after extraordinary circumstances (Cade: collapsed lung; Luka: missed games for the birth of his child plus a late-season injury). Draymond supported the decision from the start, calling the games-played threshold rule "dumb." Anthony Edwards remains ineligible; Draymond suggests the framing of his absence as "left knee injury management" — the exact language the rule was designed to prevent — likely cost him.

Draymond's personal MVP list (post-eligibility ruling): 1. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander, 2. Cade Cunningham, 3. Victor Wembanyama, 4. Nikola Jokić, 5. Luka Dončić.

**LaMelo Ball / Bam Adebayo tripping controversy**
In the Hornets-Heat playing game, LaMelo appeared to grab and trip Bam Adebayo, who fell, injured his back, and exited. Draymond's take: it was an intentional trip but not intentional injury — two distinct things. He draws a direct parallel to the Domantas Sabonis ankle-grab incident involving himself, where no fine was issued because Draymond was not hurt. He is critical of the inconsistency (outcome-based enforcement) but glad LaMelo was not suspended, since a suspension with no injury precedent would have been hypocritical.

**76ers beat Orlando, now face Celtics**
Draymond predicts Boston wins the series (see verifiable predictions above). He tells excited 76ers fans to "relax yourself — no, you don't [want Boston]."
